What is Bhagavatha

Bhagavata Purana, also known as Srimad Bhagavatam, is one of Hinduism's eighteen major Puranas. It is a Sanskrit text traditionally attributed to Veda Vyasa and primarily focuses on bhakti (devotion) towards Krishna.

Why Bhagavatha

1.Spiritual Guidance

The Bhagavata Purana emphasizes Bhakti Yoga (devotional worship) as the ultimate path to self-realization and liberation (moksha). It inspires people to cultivate love and devotion toward Krishna or the divine.

2.Tools for Meditation and Self-Reflection

Reciting or listening to the Bhagavata Purana is believed to purify the mind and soul, bringing clarity and focus. Its hymns, prayers, and philosophical discussions are excellent tools for meditation and introspection.

3.Promotes Universal Values

According to the text, merely reading or hearing the Bhagavata Purana with devotion can help one attain liberation (moksha) by fostering detachment from the material world and a deeper connection with the divine.
